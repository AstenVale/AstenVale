Briefing 01

What Is the Asten Vale Archives?

The Asten Vale Archives is an ongoing mystery told through music, recovered case files, and evidence hidden in plain hearing.

Every case begins with a song. Every song contains fragments the official record missed. When an investigation is solved, additional archive material becomes available.

"The truth is recovered, not revealed."

Briefing 02

How Investigations Work

Each file asks you to listen carefully, identify evidence, and confirm your findings before the archive opens the next layer.

Listen to the Song

Begin with the recording and treat every lyric, sound, and repeated phrase as part of the record.

Recover Seven Pieces of Evidence

Find the location, object, witness, time, marker, direction, and sound hidden inside the case.

Confirm Your Findings

Submit the evidence to the archive system and let the file verify what you recovered.

Unlock the Case File

A solved investigation opens the closed record and reveals additional material without spoiling future cases.

Continue the Investigation

Return as new files are declassified and older evidence begins to point somewhere else.

Archive NoteThe official report rarely tells the whole story.

Briefing 03

The Seven Evidence Types

Location

Where the events of the investigation occurred.

Object

A recovered artifact connected to the case.

Witness

Someone connected to the investigation or its aftermath.

Time

A significant recorded moment in the file.

Marker

A numbered clue that can link one investigation to another.

Direction

A route, instruction, or movement hidden inside the archive.

Sound

An environmental recording recovered from the investigation.

Briefing 04

The Archivist

The archive and the Archivist are not the same thing. The archive is the collection; the Archivist is the unknown hand that assembled it.

The handwriting appears throughout the files, sometimes years apart. No official record identifies who wrote the notes, why they preserved the songs, or where the remaining files were kept.

Identity: Unconfirmed

Briefing 05

Why Music?

The recordings survived when formal reports did not. Songs carried details that paperwork ignored, softened, or removed.

Listening closely reveals names, places, sounds, and patterns hidden in plain sight. In Asten Vale, music is not decoration; it is evidence.

Internal MemoSome evidence only becomes meaningful after later investigations.

Briefing 06

Archive Releases

New investigations are declassified one at a time. Each completed case expands the archive and prepares the next file for review.

Subscribers receive notice whenever a new investigation becomes available or a sealed record changes status.

ClassifiedSeveral records remain sealed.

Briefing 07

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Asten Vale?

Asten Vale is an ongoing mystery archive where songs act as case files and listeners recover the evidence inside them.

Do I have to solve every case?

No, but each solved file gives you a clearer view of the archive and the patterns connecting it.

Can I replay investigations?

Yes. Closed cases can be revisited, replayed, and reviewed for details you may have missed.

Are clues shared between cases?

Some clues stand alone; others become more important as later records are declassified.

What happens when I complete a season?

The archive marks the season complete and prepares the next sealed group of investigations for release.
